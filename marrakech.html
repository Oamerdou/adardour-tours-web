✦ The Red City

Where ancient magic
still reigns

Founded by the Almoravid dynasty in 1070, Marrakech has served as the beating heart of Morocco for nearly a millennium. Known as the "Red City" for its distinctive pink-hued buildings of pisé earth, it is a place where centuries coexist in mesmerising harmony.

The medina — a UNESCO World Heritage site since 1985 — is a labyrinth of narrow souks, ornate mosques, and hidden palaces. Beyond its walls, the Palmeraie stretches for miles, while the High Atlas mountains loom on the horizon like sleeping giants.

Marrakech is not merely a destination; it is an experience of total immersion. From the theatrical chaos of Djemaa el-Fna at sundown to the hushed courtyards of a centuries-old riad, every moment here is charged with wonder.

✦ Must-See

Marrakech Highlights

01
Djemaa el-Fna
The world's most extraordinary square — snake charmers, acrobats, storytellers, and a thousand food stalls ignite every evening in one of humanity's great spectacles.
02
Jardin Majorelle
Yves Saint Laurent's iconic cobalt-blue garden — a sanctuary of rare cacti, tranquil pools, and the Berber Museum, set amidst vibrant indigo architecture.
03
Bahia Palace
A 19th-century masterpiece of Moroccan and Andalusian architecture — eight hectares of gilded rooms, mosaic courtyards, and carved cedar ceilings of breathtaking intricacy.
04
The Souks
A city within the city — copper lanterns, hand-knotted carpets, saffron, argan oil, and centuries-old craftsmanship that exists nowhere else on earth.
05
Saadian Tombs
Hidden for centuries behind sealed walls, these lavishly decorated royal mausoleums were rediscovered in 1917 — a rare glimpse into the grandeur of the Saadian dynasty.
06
Koutoubia Mosque
The 70-metre minaret has guided travellers to Marrakech for nine centuries. Its rose gardens and the haunting call to prayer at sunset are among Morocco's defining memories.

✦ Plan Your Visit

Practical Information

🗓️

Best Time to Visit

Marrakech is glorious year-round. For ideal conditions:

  • March–May: Spring blooms, mild temperatures
  • Sept–Nov: Golden light, comfortable heat
  • Dec–Feb: Cool, quiet, occasional rain
  • June–Aug: Hot but vibrant — arrive at dawn
🏨

Where to Stay

Adardour handpicks your accommodation based on your style:

  • Boutique riads in the medina heart
  • Palmeraie luxury villas with pool
  • Gueliz design hotels for modern comfort
  • Heritage properties with private courtyards
🍽️

Food & Drink

Marrakech's cuisine is a revelation. Don't miss:

  • Pastilla — pigeon pie dusted with icing sugar
  • Tangia — slow-cooked lamb in a clay urn
  • Msemen — flaky flatbread with argan honey
  • Atay — sweet mint tea, poured from height
💡

Insider Tips

  • Arrive at the souks before 10am for calm
  • Agree on prices before entering any calèche
  • Carry small dirham notes for souk purchases
  • Most riads have quiet rooftops perfect for sunrise
  • Fridays: medina quiets during noon prayers
