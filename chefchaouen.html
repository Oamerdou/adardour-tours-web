✦ The Blue Pearl

Where every wall
is indigo

Chefchaouen — "Chaouen" to those who love it — was founded in 1471 as a fortress to repel Portuguese invasions. Jewish refugees fleeing the Spanish Inquisition settled here in 1494, and it is largely to them that we owe the mesmerising tradition of painting every wall and alley in tones of blue — a colour said to represent the sky, heaven, and the divine.

Today, the blue-washed medina tumbles down the Rif Mountains in a cascade of painted plaster, hanging bougainvillea, and babbling streams. It is one of Morocco's most photographed places — and also one of its most genuinely peaceful, where cats sleep in sun-drenched doorways and the pace of life slows to something rare and restorative.

Above the medina, the ruins of the 15th-century kasbah look out over a landscape of cedar forest and jagged limestone peaks. In the evening, the whole city turns a deeper, more intense shade of blue-violet that no photograph has ever fully captured.

✦ Must-See

ChefchaouenHighlights

01
Plaza Uta el-Hammam
The heart of the medina — a terracotta-paved square shaded by trees where locals play cards and cafés serve argan-honey pancakes at all hours.
02
The Kasbah Museum
A 15th-century fortress housing ethnographic artefacts, Andalusian gardens, and a tower with views across the entire valley of terracotta rooftops and blue walls.
03
Ras el-Ma Spring
At the edge of the medina, a cold mountain spring tumbles down mossy rocks where Rif women come to wash laundry — one of the most timelessly photogenic scenes in Morocco.
04
Textile Souks
Chefchaouen's market specialises in woven wool — brilliantly striped djellabas, blankets, and baskets that are far more artisanal and affordable than in larger cities.
05
Talassemtane National Park
Just outside town, a UNESCO biosphere reserve of ancient cedar and fir forest where Barbary macaques and rare wildflowers inhabit valleys of extraordinary beauty.
06
Spanish Mosque at Sunset
A 20-minute hike above the medina leads to a hilltop mosque — from here, the entire blue city glows beneath you as the Rif peaks catch the last of the day's light.

✦ Plan Your Visit

Practical Information

🗓️

Best Time to Visit

  • March–May: Wildflowers, waterfalls, mild warmth
  • Sept–Nov: Warm days, cool evenings, few crowds
  • Dec–Feb: Occasional snow on the peaks — magical
  • June–Aug: Busy but beautiful; hike early
🏨

Where to Stay

  • Boutique riads within the blue medina
  • Terrace guesthouses above the city rooftops
  • Mountain eco-lodges in the cedar forest
  • Historic dar mansions near Plaza Uta
🍽️

Food to Try

  • Meloui — Rif-style flaky griddle bread
  • Goat cheese with wild mountain herbs
  • Fresh fig jam from the Rif highlands
  • Harira soup in the square at dusk
📷

Photography Tips

  • Best light: 7–9am (soft and crowd-free)
  • The alley with the flower pots — ask us
  • Evening light turns walls violet-blue
  • Use a tripod for the star-over-medina shots
  • Always ask before photographing residents
