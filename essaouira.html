✦ The Windy City

Where Africa
meets the Atlantic

Essaouira — known as Mogador through much of its history — has been a crossroads of civilisations since the Phoenicians traded purple dye here three thousand years ago. Today, its UNESCO-listed medina of whitewashed walls and cobalt-painted woodwork is one of Morocco's most immediately beguiling places.

The city is defined by its wind — the alizé trade wind blows almost year-round from the north, keeping temperatures cool and filling the bay with the coloured kites of surfers and windsurfers. The enormous beach stretches south for kilometres, wild and largely unspoiled.

Essaouira has long attracted artists, musicians, and dreamers — Jimi Hendrix famously visited in 1969, and the city's Gnawa music festival remains one of Africa's great annual cultural events. This is Morocco at its most bohemian, its most open, its most itself.

✦ What to Do

Essaouira Experiences

🏄

Surf & Kitesurf

The constant Atlantic wind makes Essaouira one of Africa's top kitesurfing destinations. Surf schools line the beach for beginners and enthusiasts alike.

🎵

Gnawa Music

The mystical Gnawa tradition — descended from sub-Saharan Africa — fills the medina's squares nightly. The June festival draws artists from across the world.

🐟

Harbour & Fish Grills

The blue-painted fishing harbour is where the day's catch is landed and immediately grilled on open charcoal. Fresh sardines here are the finest in Morocco.

🌿

Argan Country

The road between Essaouira and Agadir passes through the world's only native argan forest — visit a women's cooperative and taste the oil that built a trade.

✦ Must-See

Essaouira Highlights

01
Skala de la Ville
The great sea bastion of the ramparts — where 18th-century European cannons still point out to sea and the wind carries salt spray high over the battlements.
02
The Medina Souks
Essaouira's market specialises in thuya wood — local artisans carve the gnarled root of this rare tree into jewellery boxes, chess sets, and sculptures of extraordinary beauty.
03
Diabat & Hendrix
Walk the beach south to the ruined Kasbah Hamidouch where Jimi Hendrix famously camped — the ruins stand against the Atlantic sky like a dream half-remembered.
04
The Blue Harbour
A working fishing port painted in vivid blues and whites — watch the catch being landed and immediately grilled over charcoal by fishermen who have done this for generations.
05
Iles Purpuraires
Three islands just offshore where the Romans once produced imperial purple dye — today a protected reserve and nesting site for rare Eleonora's falcons.
06
Sunset on the Ramparts
Essaouira's sunsets are legendary — from the top of the Skala, the sky turns extraordinary shades of pink and amber above a silver sea as seagulls wheel below.

✦ Plan Your Visit

Practical Information

🗓️

Best Time to Visit

  • April–June: Warm, manageable winds
  • July–Aug: Peak surf season, lively
  • Sept–Oct: Ideal all-round conditions
  • Dec–Feb: Quietest and most atmospheric
🏨

Where to Stay

  • Boutique riads in the medina
  • Converted merchant houses on the ramparts
  • Atlantic-facing guesthouses in Diabat
  • Modern boutique hotels near the beach
🍽️

Food to Try

  • Grilled sardines at the harbour stalls
  • Amlou — argan oil, almonds, and honey
  • Pastilla au lait — flaky pastry with cream
  • Chermoula — local spiced fish marinade
💡

Insider Tips

  • Always bring a windproof layer — always
  • The Gnawa music festival is in late June
  • Shop at cooperatives, not tourist shops
  • Walk south of the city at low tide
  • Book surf lessons in advance in summer
