✦ The Great Erg

A sea of golden
silence

The Erg Chebbi is Morocco's most spectacular corner of the Sahara — a vast ocean of apricot and gold sand dunes that shifts and reshapes itself with every wind. Here, the immensity of the desert becomes visceral.

Rising from the flat hammada plain near the village of Merzouga, the dunes reach heights of 150 metres. At sunrise and sunset, the light performs an alchemy on the sand, transforming it from pale cream to burnished copper to molten amber in the space of twenty minutes.

To sleep here under an unpolluted sky — one containing an estimated 2,500 visible stars — is to understand why ancient civilisations looked upward for meaning. The Sahara does not merely show you nature; it returns you to it.

✦ Places to Explore

Desert Highlights

01
Erg Chebbi Dunes
The great sand sea itself — 22 km long and 5 km wide, the dunes are the undisputed star of the Moroccan Sahara and change colour by the hour.
02
Ksar of Ait Benhaddou
A UNESCO World Heritage fortified village of earthen towers — used as the backdrop for Lawrence of Arabia, Gladiator, and Game of Thrones alike.
03
Dayet Srji Lake
A seasonal salt lake at the foot of the dunes where pink flamingos gather in winter — one of the Sahara's most surprising and beautiful secrets.
04
Gnawa Music Nights
Around the campfire, Gnawa musicians from Merzouga play their ancestral rhythms — a hypnotic spiritual music that pulses long into the desert night.
05
Nomadic Families
Visit with semi-nomadic Amazigh families who still move with their flocks through the desert — share their bread, their stories, their ancient wisdom.
06
Sunrise from the Summit
Climb the highest dune before dawn. As the sun breaks the horizon, shadows sculpt the sand into infinite ridges — the most spectacular 15 minutes in Morocco.

✦ Plan Your Visit

Practical Information

🗓️

Best Time to Visit

  • Oct-Apr: Ideal — cool nights, warm days
  • Mar: Desert wildflowers after winter rain
  • Dec-Jan: Cold nights but magical atmosphere
  • May-Sept: Very hot; arrive before sunrise
⛺

Camp Accommodation

  • Luxury canvas tents with real beds
  • Private en-suite bathroom facilities
  • Traditional Berber décor and lanterns
  • Communal dining tent for starlit dinners
🎒

What to Bring

  • Warm layers — nights drop to 5°C in winter
  • Sunscreen SPF 50+ and UV sunglasses
  • Headscarf for wind and sand
  • Headtorch for pre-dawn dune climbs
  • Camera with wide-angle lens for stars
💡

Insider Tips

  • Book October-November for ideal conditions
  • Request a wake-up at 5:30am for the summit
  • Leave your phone on the dune — just watch
  • The February Merzouga Rally adds desert energy
